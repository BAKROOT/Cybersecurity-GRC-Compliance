## 1. Purpose

Defines how risks are scored so that assessments are consistent and comparable.

## 2. Likelihood Scale

| Score | Rating | Meaning |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Rare | Unlikely in the next few years |
| 2 | Unlikely | Could occur but not expected |
| 3 | Possible | Might occur at some point |
| 4 | Likely | Expected to occur |
| 5 | Almost Certain | Expected to occur frequently |

## 3. Impact Scale

| Score | Rating | Meaning |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Negligible | Minimal effect |
| 2 | Minor | Limited, short-term effect |
| 3 | Moderate | Noticeable operational or financial effect |
| 4 | Major | Significant harm, regulatory attention |
| 5 | Severe | Critical harm to operations, finances, or reputation |

## 4. Risk Score

Risk Score = Likelihood x Impact (range 1-25).

| Score Range | Band | Response |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1-4 | Low | Accept or monitor |
| 5-9 | Medium | Manage with planned controls |
| 10-15 | High | Time-bound treatment plan required |
| 16-25 | Critical | Immediate action; leadership notified |

## 5. Inherent vs Residual

Inherent risk is scored before existing controls. Residual risk is scored after accounting for controls and treatment.

## 6. Re-assessment

Risks are re-assessed after treatment, after incidents, and at least at each scheduled review.
