## 1. Purpose

To define how the organization identifies, assesses, treats, monitors, and reports information-security risk in a consistent and repeatable way.

## 2. Risk Appetite

Leadership sets the level of risk the organization is willing to accept. As a default position: Critical risks are not acceptable and require immediate action; High risks require a time-bound treatment plan; Medium risks are managed; Low risks may be accepted.

## 3. Risk Management Lifecycle

1. Establish context - assets, threats, obligations, and scope.
2. Identify risk - from assessments, audits, incidents, threat intel, and change.
3. Analyze and evaluate - score likelihood and impact (see methodology).
4. Treat - mitigate, transfer, avoid, or accept.
5. Monitor and review - track treatment and re-assess.
6. Communicate - report status to stakeholders and leadership.

## 4. Treatment Options

| Option | Description |
| --- | --- |
| Mitigate | Apply controls to reduce likelihood or impact |
| Transfer | Share risk via insurance or contracts |
| Avoid | Stop the activity that creates the risk |
| Accept | Formally accept residual risk with owner sign-off |

## 5. Roles

| Role | Responsibility |
| --- | --- |
| Risk Owner | Accountable for treating and accepting a specific risk |
| Security Team | Facilitate assessments and maintain the register |
| CISO | Own the framework and report on risk |
| Leadership | Set appetite and accept residual risk |

## 6. Register

All risks are recorded in risk-register.csv with an owner, score, treatment, and review date.

## 7. Review

The framework is reviewed at least annually.
