# Risk Management

The risk domain holds everything needed to **identify, assess, treat, and monitor** information-security risk. It centers on a living risk register supported by a documented framework, methodology, and reusable templates.

## Files

| Path | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| risk-management-framework.md | The overall risk management approach, governance, and appetite |
| risk-register.csv | The living register of identified risks and their treatment |
| methodology/risk-assessment-methodology.md | How likelihood and impact are scored and combined |
| templates/risk-assessment-template.md | A blank template for assessing a new risk or initiative |

## Risk Process

1. Identify - capture risks from assessments, incidents, audits, and change.
2. Assess - score likelihood and impact using the methodology.
3. Treat - choose to mitigate, transfer, avoid, or accept each risk.
4. Monitor - track treatment progress and re-assess regularly.

## Scoring Model

Risk = Likelihood x Impact, each rated 1 (low) to 5 (high), producing a 1-25 score that maps to Low, Medium, High, or Critical bands. See the methodology for details.

## Cadence

The register is reviewed continuously and formally at least quarterly. High and Critical risks are reported to leadership.
