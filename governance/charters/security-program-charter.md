## 1. Purpose

This charter establishes the Information Security Program and grants it the authority, resources, and mandate to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of organizational information assets.

## 2. Scope

The program applies to all employees, contractors, systems, applications, facilities, and third parties that store, process, or transmit organizational information.

## 3. Mission and Objectives

The program exists to manage information-security risk to an acceptable level, meet legal and contractual obligations, and enable the business to operate securely. Objectives include protecting assets, ensuring compliance, responding to incidents, and continuously improving the security posture.

## 4. Authority

The CISO is authorized to define policies and standards, require remediation of identified risks, approve or deny exceptions, and escalate unresolved risks to executive leadership.

## 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| --- | --- |
| Board / Executive Leadership | Approve the program, accept residual risk, allocate resources |
| CISO | Own and run the program; report on risk and compliance |
| Security Team | Implement controls, monitor, and respond to incidents |
| Control Owners | Operate and maintain assigned controls |
| All Personnel | Comply with policies and report security concerns |

## 6. Governance and Reporting

The program reports security posture, key risks, and compliance status to executive leadership on a defined cadence (at least quarterly).

## 7. Review

This charter is reviewed at least annually and updated to reflect changes in the business, regulatory environment, or risk landscape.
