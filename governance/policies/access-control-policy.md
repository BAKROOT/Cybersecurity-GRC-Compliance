## 1. Purpose

To ensure that access to information and systems is granted, managed, and revoked based on business need and the principles of least privilege and segregation of duties.

## 2. Scope

Applies to all systems, applications, data stores, and accounts (user, privileged, service, and third-party).

## 3. Principles

- Least privilege: grant the minimum access necessary to perform a role.
- Need to know: access is tied to a legitimate business requirement.
- Segregation of duties: split conflicting responsibilities across people.
- Accountability: every action is attributable to a unique identity.

## 4. Access Lifecycle

- Provisioning: access is requested, approved by the resource owner, and granted via a documented process.
- Modification: changes follow the same approval process; role changes trigger access review.
- Deprovisioning: access is revoked promptly upon termination or role change.

## 5. Authentication

Unique credentials are required for every user. Multi-factor authentication is required for remote access, privileged access, and access to sensitive systems. Password requirements are defined in the Password Standard.

## 6. Privileged Access

Privileged accounts are limited, individually assigned where possible, logged, and reviewed regularly.

## 7. Access Reviews

User and privileged access is reviewed at least quarterly per the Access Review Procedure.

## 8. Review

Reviewed at least annually.
